Descriptions

dedicated to empowering Africa’s creatives with the digital, entrepreneurial, and technical skills necessary for success in the global marketplace. Our mission is to build a thriving ecosystem of highly skilled creative professionals who are digitally empowered and globally competitive, ready to drive economic growth and digital inclusion across the continent. We recognize that talent alone is not enough, as many young creatives face challenges like limited mentorship, business knowledge, and access to resources or funding. By addressing these barriers, KHUB aims to unlock Africa’s creative potential, fostering innovation and shaping impactful storytelling in fields such as film, photography, events, entertainment, art, and technology. The

Programs

01 The Creative Master Class

is an intensive five-week training program designed to sharpen creatives' business acumen, professional conduct, and technical skills. Participants engage in four weekly modules covering business development, communications, film, and photography, equipping them with the knowledge and discipline necessary for successful careers. The program also nurtures a supportive community, offering resources, experienced mentors, and job opportunities to help young creatives and entrepreneurs transform their talent into structured, profitable careers.

02 The Journey to Self Mastery

is a three-month online mentorship program designed to guide young people in their journey of self-discovery. The program focuses on helping participants develop discipline, set and achieve their year goals, and ultimately work towards their personal and professional success.

03 The Computer Skills Program

is tailored to equip university students and recent graduates with essential computer skills and foundational knowledge. Participants receive hands-on training and coaching in Microsoft Word, Excel, email, and internet use from an instructor with 40 years of experience, helping them enhance their professional competency. In partnership with American Corner and the U.S. Embassy, the program offers access to computers, WiFi, and expert guidance, empowering students and young professionals to excel in their careers.

04 Khub Film Academy - Screen Bongo!

A six-month film academy developed with the Tanzanian Film Board and African Film Commission to address gaps in film education and production in Tanzania. It provides training, facilities, and a platform for local storytelling, boosting employment and cultural representation in the Tanzanian film industry. The academy aims to empower African storytellers and contribute to the growth of the African creative economy.
